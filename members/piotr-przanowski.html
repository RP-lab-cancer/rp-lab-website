Dr. Piotr Przanowski is a Polish molecular biologist whose research spans cancer biology, genomics, epigenetics, and immunology. During his international Ph.D. thesis, he investigated the role of transcription factors STAT1 and STAT3 in regulating JMJD3 and UBA7 as key mediators of neuroinflammation, supported by a Preludium Predoctoral Grant from the National Science Center (Poland), in the laboratories of Dr. Bożena Kamińska-Kaczmarek from Nencki Institute of Experimental Biology (Warsaw, Poland), Dr. Arkadiusz Piotrowski at Gdansk Medical University, Dr. Helmut Kettenmann at the Max Delbrück Center (Berlin, Germany), and Dr. Jan Komorowski at Rudbeck Laboratory (Uppsala, Sweden). His early postdoctoral work focused on pharmacological reactivation of the silenced X chromosome, the role of oncogenic TRIM37 in breast cancer chemotherapy resistance and metastasis, and production of bispecific antibodies for ovarian cancer therapies. After transitioning to a Research Scientist position, he investigated the role of ANKLE1 in breast cancer susceptibility and the breast tumor microenvironment, with a particular focus on distinct premalignant cell states and their crosstalk with the surrounding normal tissue.
